About us

Written for this chapter — not the last one.

Purposeful Living After 60 exists for one reason: to help you keep living with energy, security, and meaning in the years that too many people are told to simply "wind down." We think this chapter can be the fullest one yet.

Our mission

Honest, warm, and genuinely useful

Most advice for people over 60 is either sugar-coated or scary. It either pretends nothing changes, or treats every year past 60 as a problem to be managed. We believe there's a better, truer voice — one that respects your intelligence and your experience.

"Purpose doesn't retire. It just changes shape."

Everything we publish sits under three simple ideas: feel good, plan well, and stay engaged. In practice, that means three areas — Health & Vitality, Retirement & Purpose, and Digital Income — each covered with articles practical enough to act on this week and warm enough to make you feel seen.

What we stand for

The promises we make to you

Practical over preachy

Real steps you can take, not vague inspiration. If an article can't help you do something differently this week, we don't publish it.

Honest, always

No hype, no fear-mongering, and no promises we can't keep. Where we recommend a product or resource, we tell you plainly — including when a link may earn us a small commission.

For everyone in this chapter

Every background, every body, every story, every kind of second act. However your life looks, there is a place for you here. Our language and our images are chosen to welcome, never to exclude.

The people behind it

Who writes this

Purposeful Living After 60 is produced by a small editorial team who research each topic carefully, draw on reputable health and financial sources, and write in plain, respectful language. We're not here to replace your doctor, your financial adviser, or your own good judgement — we're here to be the friend a few steps ahead, pointing out what's worked for others and where to look next.

Our editorial standards

We take accuracy seriously. Health guidance is written to be safe and general, with clear reminders to check with your doctor before making changes — and we never make medical guarantees. Financial and income topics are presented as education, not personal advice, and we always encourage speaking to a qualified professional before big decisions. We review our articles for clarity and correctness, update them as things change, and disclose any commercial relationships openly. When we get something wrong, we fix it. You can read exactly how we handle your data, our use of advertising, and our affiliate relationships on our privacy and disclosure page.

Say hello

Whether you're 60 and just starting to think about what's next, or 80 and living proof that the best years aren't behind you, we're glad you're here.
